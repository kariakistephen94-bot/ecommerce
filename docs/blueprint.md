# **App Name**: Hot Ice Fashion

## Core Features:

- Product Catalog & Filtering: Browse a grid of fashion products with filtering (price, category) and sorting options.
- Detailed Product View: View individual product pages featuring image galleries, descriptions, size selection, and quantity input.
- Shopping Cart Management: Add, remove, and update quantities of items in a simulated shopping cart using local storage for persistent state.
- Simulated Checkout Process: Experience a complete, simulated checkout flow with form fields for personal details, payment options, and an order summary leading to a success confirmation.
- Responsive Navigation & Page Routing: Seamlessly navigate between homepage, shop, product, cart, about, and contact pages, ensuring full responsiveness across all devices.
- AI Outfit & Styling Suggestion Tool: Provides AI-driven suggestions for complementary fashion items and styling tips based on the product currently being viewed.

## Style Guidelines:

- Light background scheme, establishing a clean, luxury aesthetic. Primary interactive and textual elements will use a sophisticated dark blue-grey (#43434C), ensuring strong contrast. The main background will be a very subtle, almost white, light blue-grey (#F5F5F7). A soft, elegant blue (#69A1CB) will serve as an accent for highlights and calls-to-action.
- A single modern sans-serif font, 'Inter', will be used for both headlines and body text to maintain a minimalist, sleek, and contemporary feel. Its versatility supports bold weights for impactful headlines and readability for product descriptions.
- Clean, line-based, and minimalist icons will be utilized to complement the luxury aesthetic and prevent visual clutter, aligning with the brand's sophisticated image.
- A mobile-first, fully responsive design prioritizing ample whitespace, grid-based product displays, and clear information hierarchy for a luxurious and intuitive user experience across all screen sizes.
- Subtle and smooth animations will enhance user interaction without distraction. Examples include elegant fade-ins for content loading and gentle hover effects on interactive elements like product cards and buttons, contributing to a refined user flow.
